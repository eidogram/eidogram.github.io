I am Riccardo Scalco, an italian independent designer and consultant in Data Analysis and Visualization.

My background is in Theoretical Physics and I completed a PhD in Computational Biochemistry.

I operate on the research activities within private and public institutions, giving support to the visualization of patterns emerging from complex phenomena and to the interaction with highly organized datasets.

My approach to the data visualization has its roots on the crossroad between the abstract mathematical thinking and the guidelines drawn by the semiotic of graphics. I therefore offer a peculiar expertise on the creation of expressive graphical representations of complex scenarios.

Frequently Asked Questions

    What programming languages and software do you use?
    I use mainly Web Standards (HTML-CSS-SVG), Javascript and Python.

    Do you use charts libraries?
    No. I use mainly d3.js because I usually create interactive graphs tailored to the customer needs. A good visualization is designed around data.

    Do you need the data?
    Generally not, but I need to know how data is organized. For example, a sample of real (or random) data is a good starting point.

    Can you afford short deadlines?
    Usually yes, when with "short" I mean one or two weeks.

    Are you a data scientist?
    I am at ease with (big) data, I have a background in Science and a broad experience in Python scripting. Have a look at my scientific papers [1,2,3], my Python library on Markov Chains and some other projects.

    How can I contact you?
    Send an email to riccardo.scalco@gmail.com, I will reply shortly after.

# Network Meta Analysis Data

Project financed by the Piedmont Health care system in relation with the Mario Negri Institute for Pharmacological Research. The project is currently under development.

Design and development of the graphical representation. Tools used: D3js. In collaboration with Zadig and Tuxtax.

# Influenza Virus Evolution

Project started in collaboration with the Department of Physics at the University of Parma. The project is under development and its goal is the creation af a visualization tool for the research activities on the time evolution of Influenza viruses.

Design and development of the graphical representation. Conception and analysis in collaboration with R.Burioni and M.Casartelli. Tools used: D3js and Python.

# Uncertainty on Voter Elections

Project developed on the occasion of the Piemonte Visual Contest, it is a case example of use of Open Data for analysis of social interest.

Conception, analysis, design, development. Tools used: D3js and Python. In collaboration with Tuxtax.

# Statistics on Infectious Diseases

Project financed by the SSepi-SeREMI epidemiology service on monitoring and prevention of infectious diseases, in collaboration with the Zadig editorial company.

Design and development of graphic representations. Tools used: Web Standards and D3js.

# Telecom Call Detail Records

Analysis performed on the occasion of the Telecom Big Data Challenge. A sample of 53 millions of strings of data have been analyzed by means of a normalization mechanism targeted to find space-time patterns on the user behaviour throughout the working-day in the city of Milan.

Conception, analysis. Tools used: Python.

# Stardust: a linkblog template powered by Meteor

Stardust is a collaborative linkblog, you have the opportunity to suggest links and to upvote links suggested by others. It is powered by Meteor, an ultra-simple environment for building top-quality web apps.

tools: Meteor, Mongodb.

# Pykov: finite Markov chains with Python

Pykov is versatile python module useful to manipulate Markov chains. It is able to calculate various kind of quantities, like steady state distribution, mean first passage times, random walks, absorbing times, and so on.

tools: python.

# A tangram tribute

# Latest earthquakes in Italy

This is a continuously updated webpage showing the latest earthquakes in Italy and neighboring regions. Earthquakes are classified according their place, date, magnitude and depth.

data source: INGV.
tools: Scrapy, Python and Kartograph.

# Where would you send your mother-in-law?

This project is an hilarious example of collaborative dataviz: the user is asked to insert some data and, once inserted, the visualization will be synchronously updated all over the world. In other words, the user interacts both with the visualization and the database.

data source: You, Stamen Design and Openstreetmap.
tools: Leaflet, Meteor and Inkscape.
Source code on github.

# Science, Technology and Innovation in Italy

The map shows the role, in terms of innovation, played by the italian administrative regions. The statistics is presented by means of a cartogram, where the area of every region is proportional to the corresponding innovation effort.

data source: istat.it.
tools: topojson, d3-cartogram, D3js and Inkscape.

# Positive and negative emotions on Twitter

The italian words 'amo', odio', 'felice', 'triste' ('love', 'hate', 'happy', 'sad') and others have been tracked on the public Twitter streaming during an ordinary Friday. Not a news, but still it's amazing how people freely express the most private feelings on social media.

tools: D3js, Twython and Dataset.
