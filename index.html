# Piedmont municipalities: population density vs voter turnout

The voter turnout has been related with the population density of the 1206 municipalities of the italian region Piedmont. Moreover, the voter uncertainty is calculated by means of the Shannon entropy on the number of votes assigned to every delegate.

data source: interno.it, istat.it.
tools: D3js.

by the Tuxtax team

# Telecom Call Detail Records

The following is a mathematical analysis of about 53280000 Telecom CDRs records. The main goal is the creation of a data model that can provide richer information on basic trends on customer usage, by means of a cost-effective mechanism that normalize the data according to relevant space and time dependences of the service consumption.

data source: telecomitalia.com
tools: Python, D3js.

by the Tuxtax team

# People displaced by natural disasters during 2012

In 2012, an estimated 32.4 million people in 82 countries were newly displaced by disasters, and the vast majority of displacement is triggered by climate and weather-related hazards in north-east India and Nigeria.

# Rohmetric: investigate Influenza virus evolution with entropic metric measures

The project aims to investigate the evolution of Influenza Virus by means of cutting edge mathematical methods. Different virus strains are identifying by horizontal colored groups, each point identifies the hemagglutinin segment of an isolate, isolates are full lenght and come from all over the world.

data source: GISAID.
tools: Python, Numpy, D3js, and Google charts.

# Pykov: finite Markov chains with Python

Pykov is versatile python module useful to manipulate Markov chains. It is able to calculate various kind of quantities, like steady state distribution, mean first passage times, random walks, absorbing times, and so on.

tools: python.

# Where would you send your mother-in-law?

This project is an hilarious example of collaborative dataviz: the user is asked to insert some data and, once inserted, the visualization will be synchronously updated all over the world. In other words, the user interacts both with the visualization and the database.

data source: You, Stamen Design and Openstreetmap.
tools: Leaflet, Meteor and Inkscape.
Source code on github.

# Science, Technology and Innovation in Italy

The map shows the role, in terms of innovation, played by the italian administrative regions. The statistics is presented by means of a cartogram, where the area of every region is proportional to the corresponding innovation effort.

data source: istat.it.
tools: topojson, d3-cartogram, D3js and Inkscape.

# Positive and negative emotions on Twitter

The italian words 'amo', odio', 'felice', 'triste' ('love', 'hate', 'happy', 'sad') and others have been tracked on the public Twitter streaming during an ordinary Friday. Not a news, but still it's amazing how people freely express the most private feelings on social media.

tools: D3js, Twython and Dataset.
