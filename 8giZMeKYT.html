Entropy analysis to discover uncertainty in election results.

A snap general election was held in Italy on 13–14 April 2008. The following maps show the winner party and the uncertainty to win for every Italian comune (the basic administrative division in Italy).

The winner is identifying as the party with the largest number of votes.

This map could be misleading because the actual victory margin is completely hidden. To have an idea of the uncertainty to win the following entropy analysis could help.

The uncertainty is calculated by means of the Shannon entropy on the number of votes assigned to every party. For example, given the comune let’s suppose that the three parties , and obtained , and votes (the total number of votes is ). Then the uncertainty is calculated as the Shannon entropy :

On the map, low and high values mean low and high uncertainty to win. In other words, for a comune with low uncertainty it is easier to estimate who is the winner than a comune with high uncertainty.
